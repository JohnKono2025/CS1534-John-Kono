Larnaca, a historic coastal city in Cyprus

Nestled along the southern coast of Cyprus, Larnaca is a city steeped in history and culture. Its story dates back over 6,000 years, making it one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Originally known as Kition, Larnaca was an important ancient city-kingdom founded by the Mycenaeans in the 13th century BCE. It became a major center for trade and commerce, largely due to its strategic location and bustling port.

During the classical period, Larnaca thrived under Greek and later Roman influence, becoming a hub for art, philosophy, and learning. The city's prominence continued through the Byzantine era, where it was known for its significant religious landmarks, including the stunning Church of Saint Lazarus. According to tradition, Saint Lazarus, who was resurrected by Jesus, settled in Larnaca and became its first bishop.

The city's fortunes ebbed and flowed with the tides of history. Under Ottoman rule from the 16th century, Larnaca served as a vital administrative and trade center. This era brought about a rich cultural tapestry, evident in the blend of architecture and customs that survive to this day. The arrival of British colonial rule in the late 19th century ushered in modern developments, transforming Larnaca into a cosmopolitan city while preserving its historical charm.

Today, Larnaca is a vibrant destination known for its beautiful seafront promenade, the bustling Finikoudes Beach, and the picturesque Salt Lake, home to flocks of flamingos. Visitors can explore its historical sites, enjoy its lively atmosphere, and immerse themselves in the blend of ancient and modern that defines this captivating city.

Whether you're strolling through its ancient ruins or savoring the local cuisine, Larnaca offers a unique journey through time and a warm Cypriot welcome.

Church of Saint Lazarus:

This late-9th century church is named after Lazarus of Bethany, who, according to tradition, fled to Cyprus after being resurrected by Jesus. He was appointed as the first Bishop of Kition and lived for thirty more years. The church was built over his reputed second tomb.

Larnaca Port:

This port has been a significant hub for trade and commerce since ancient times. The Venetian Port of Larnaca, in particular, played a crucial role during the Venetian rule in Cyprus, serving as a vital trade link between the island and various Mediterranean destinations.

Pierides Museum:

This is the oldest private museum in Cyprus. It houses a collection initiated by Demetrios Pierides in the 19th century, aimed at preserving Cyprus' antiquities. The museum is located in the old residence of the Pierides family, a colonial-style building built in 1825.

Larnaca Castle:

Larnaca Castle, located on the southern coast of Cyprus. Over the centuries, it has served various purposes, including an artillery station, a prison, and now a museum. The castle's architecture reflects various influences from different periods of occupation.

Hala Sultan Tekke:

This mosque and tekke is located on the west of Larnaca Salt Lake. Mosque built in honor of Umm Haram, known as Hala Sultan in Turkish tradition, who was a companion of the Prophet Muhammad. The complex includes a mosque, mausoleum & cemetery.
